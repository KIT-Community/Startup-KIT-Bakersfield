About

Startup KIT Bakersfield is a 61 day mini-accelerator program and competition aimed at early stage tech startup ideas. This program is designed to help take projects from the idea stage to market ready products. All accelerator teams will be entered to compete for a large grand prize (at this point, we are raising funds for a $10,000 prize). Preference for this accelerator is given to ideas focused on social good and environmental issues.

This accelerator is a program of the KIT Community in partnership with the Bakersfield Mayor's Office, Kern County Libraries, CSUB Small Business Development Center, MESH Cowork, and local investors.

Any questions can be addressed to Alyssa Haas at the KIT Community at contact@followthefox.org

Activities

• Opening and Closing Event

• Regular Weekly Workshops (6-7 in total, topics tbd)

• Mentor/Coach Weekly Office Hours

Rules

• Teams must register in advance of the program by the deadline

• Project Ideas will be minimally vetted to ensure related to tech and feasible to complete minimum viable product during event time period

• Half the team has to be physically located in Kern County during the event

• Ideas and code may be worked on in advance, but this competition is designed for early stage ideas. Fully or partially completed projects that are already significantly progressed may be rejected. Significant features addition to existing projects, or new project directions (pivot) for an existing development team are okay.

• Ideas that have already received angel, investor, or venture capital funding will not be accepted. Family, friends, and self-funded ideas are okay.

• Any idea developed or built for a KIT hosted or co-hosted hackathon are automatically accepted, assuming the team applying is largely composed of the team that was competing during the previous hackathon.

• Winning team must form a corporation or legal entity in Bakersfield to accept prize money. Prize money will be awarded to this legal entity as seed capital.
